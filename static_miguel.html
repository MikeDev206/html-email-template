Welcome to the Resy Lineup, your weekly dose of recommendations on what to eat, drink and see. In this week's edition: a tavern with an improbable wine list, a farewell to a proper New York standbym and a food crawl through Chinatown.

What to Eat & Drink

The Restaurant recommendation

Chaiwali, because after a two-year rennovation, Harlem's excellent Indian restaurant has reopened - and the butter chicken is among the best takes the city has ever known.

The Drink Proposal

A bottle of Château Moulin de Tricot Margaux from Red Hook Tavern. The old-school Bordeaux is one of the many stellar offerings from the Tavern’s thoughtful wine program.

What to See & Do

The Stay-At-Home Move

It’s movie night and you’ve got your go-to pizza joint on speed dial, but why not try Lebanon’s spin on the pie? In the East Village, Balade kneads fresh dough for its manakeesh — a bready, chewy 8-inch disc topped with goodness (get the wild zaatar or the lahme baajin).

The Event Invite

Thursday, August 27 marks the end of Bar Sardine, and you’re invited to bid adieu to this corner fixture the only way you should: with a platter of oysters, a round (or two) of cocktails, and your last Fedora burger.

The Weekend Pairing

There’s no better activity for a sunny weekend than food crawling in Chinatown. The last stretch of Mott Street has been closed for outdoor dining, meaning you can sample your way through dim sum, soup dumplings, and stir-fried lobster, among other tasty things.
